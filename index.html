What is Fundu?

Sport. Ritual. Movement.

Fundu is a cooperative movement practice built around a shared geometric pattern — two interlocking figure-eights, seven players, and the collective goal of keeping two balls flowing without a drop. Nobody wins alone. Nobody loses alone.

Think of it as the intersection of a pickup game, a movement ritual, and a social experience. Phones down. Eyes up. Real connection.

🌀
The Game

7 players. 2 balls. One shared figure-eight pattern. Cooperative scoring — everyone succeeds or resets together.

🌊
The Feeling

A flow state you can only find together. Clean rhythm, genuine laughter, and the satisfaction of a pattern clicking into place.

⚡
The Vision

Fundu is designed to scale like pickleball or yoga — playable weekly with friends, families, and communities worldwide.

How to Play

01

Setup

7 players take the field inside a 30-foot circle. Players 1–5 are Anchors at fixed positions. Players 6 and 7 are Movers — they each start outside the circle holding one ball.

02

Go

On the countdown, both Movers enter the circle and begin running their interlocking figure-eight paths. Both paths cross at Position 5 — the ignition point at the center. The 20-second timer begins.

03

Flow

Mover A loops 1 → 5 → 3 → 5. Mover B loops 2 → 5 → 4 → 5. Both balls cross at the center. Any player may break position to recover a dropped ball. The goal is unbroken flow for the full 20 seconds.

04

Rotate & Repeat

After each round, everyone shifts one position. 7 rounds total — every player experiences every role, including center. Reset takes 10 seconds. Then go again.
